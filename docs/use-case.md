# StudentCoach Use Case

## Student Problem

Students often need to manage lectures, assignments, exams, personal routines, work shifts, and focus time at the same time. These responsibilities are usually spread across calendars, notes, messaging apps, and task lists.

The result is often mental overload: students know they have many things to do, but it is hard to understand what matters now, what can wait, and how to begin.

## Why Students Need This Tool

Students need a planning tool that supports both productivity and emotional clarity. A useful student assistant should not only list tasks. It should help the student feel oriented, reduce pressure, and make the next step obvious.

StudentCoach is designed for that need. It brings academic planning, personal tasks, weekly scheduling, focus sessions, and motivation into one Hebrew RTL experience.

## How StudentCoach Solves the Problem

StudentCoach combines several student workflows:

- A calm daily dashboard for seeing the day at a glance
- A weekly diary for academic, personal, and focus events
- A task manager with simple categories and progress
- A personal area for habits, settings, and weekly balance
- A gamification center with XP, achievements, and Pomodoro focus tools

Instead of treating productivity as a long list of obligations, StudentCoach turns it into a guided daily rhythm.

## Main User Journey

1. The student opens the dashboard / לוח בקרה.
2. They see a friendly greeting and a coach check-in.
3. They review the daily flow: academic events, personal events, and focus blocks.
4. They add or review tasks in the task manager / מנהל משימות.
5. They use the weekly diary / יומן שבועי to understand their week.
6. When they need focus, they click the floating robot assistant.
7. The robot opens the focus center / מרכז הפוקוס.
8. The student starts a Pomodoro session and earns XP after completing focus work.

## Example User Story

As a student, I want to see my daily academic and personal tasks in one calm dashboard, so I can focus without feeling overwhelmed.

## Gamification and Motivation

Gamification gives students small positive feedback loops. XP points, levels, and achievements make progress visible even when large academic goals still feel far away.

In StudentCoach, gamification is intentionally lightweight. It supports motivation without turning the app into a distracting game.

## Pomodoro and Focus

The Pomodoro timer helps students start work in a manageable way. A 25-minute focus block feels less intimidating than an open-ended study session.

After completing a Pomodoro session, the app displays an XP reward message. This connects focus behavior with immediate positive reinforcement.

## Floating Robot Assistant

The floating robot creates a friendly assistant experience. It is always visible, but it does not dominate the interface.

The robot is the entry point to focus mode, XP, achievements, and Pomodoro sessions. This makes the focus center feel approachable and gives the app a memorable product identity.
